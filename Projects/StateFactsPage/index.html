The capital city of Idaho is Boise which is where I was born.

Cons to living in Idaho

  1. High cost of living
    • The cost of living in Boise, Idaho is compariable to Salem, Oregon
  2. Low salary
    • My dad works at the Oregon jail who makes almost $50 per hour.
      He would make around $25 per hour in Idaho for the exact same job.
    • Low pay with a cost of living comparable to Salem makes living there difficult.
  3. Harsh Weather
    • During the summer, most of Idaho is very hot. It the 90's and 100's.
    • While it's winter, it's always very cold and snows a lot.



Why I love Idaho

For these reasons, my parents decided to move to Oregon. I would only recommend living in
Boise if you have a lot of money. If you have money, it's a beautiful place
to live.

There isn't really any homeless people in sight. Whenever you go to a public park in
Salem, it's littered with homeless camps and children can't go to the parks because it's not safe.
That's why the only park my mom would ever take me to was a school playground.

They also keep the city very clean and organized. There isn't any trash in sight and
the roads are maintained very well. The paint is replaced pretty often. Last summer, my mom's
boyfriend went to Boise for the first time on our way to Yellowstone. The moment he
got there, he loved it much better than Salem. He also loved the fact that highway
speed limits were 80mph.
