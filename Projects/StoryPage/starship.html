Starship

Starship is the largest and most powerful rocket ever built, also one of the cheapest only costing around $60 million per launch. That's cheap compared to Artemis which is $4 billion per launch.

Starship provides over 16 million pounds of force (lbf) and 398ft tall, even then, the rocket is planned to get 20% taller than this.

Below is a table comparing Starship to other rockets. Artemis is NASA's superheavy rocket, the successor of Apollo in returning humans to the moon. SaturnV is NASA's superheavy rocket that landed humans on the moon 1969-1972. These are the three largest rockets ever launched.

Operations Height (ft) Thrust (lbf) Cost Per Launch (2024) Program Cost
SaturnV 1967-1973 363 7.6 millon $1.5 billion $200.4 billion
Artemis 2022-present 322 8.8 million $4 billion $93 billion (by 2025)
Starship 2023-present 398 17 million $60 million $5-10 billion

SpaceX Catches Starship Booster

SpaceX accomplished a historic achievement when they caught Starship's booster at the launch pad going faster than the speed of sound. Elon Musk rated to booster catch to have a 50% chance of success. This is an important aspect for its launch turnarounds and reusable capabilities.



(CC bgnessnews.com) Starship fly's through the air

Starship lifted off for the fifth time October 13, 2024. SpaceX was going to attempt for a tower catch on the launch pad for the first time ever. Elon Musk gave this attempt a 50% chance of success.

The launch pad tower is equipped with two arms that are supposed to come out when the booster returns to the launch pad to land. This is important for the reusability and turnaround for Starship launches. Watch a video of the booster catch here.
